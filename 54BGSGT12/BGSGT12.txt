BGSGT12

BGSGT12 means "British Geological Survey Geological Timescale 2012", which has 1583 triples. Covers the geological duration from 0 to 4600 Ma. Includes 4 Eons (Phanerozoic, Proterozoic, Archaean and
Hadean), 10 Eras (Cenozoic, Mesozoic, Palaeozoic, Palaeoproterozoic, Mesoproterozoic, Neoproterozoic, Eoarchaean, Palaeoarchaean, Mesoarchaean, Neoarchaene), 22 Periods (Neogene, Palaeogene, Quaternary,
Cretaceous, Jurassic, Triassic, Permian, Carboniferous, Devonian, Ordovician, Silurian, Cambrian, Ediacaran, Cryogenian, Tonian, Stenian, Ectasian, Calymmian, Statherian, Orosirian, Rhyacian, Siderian)
) and 36 Epoches (Pleistocene, Holocene, Miocene, Pliocene, Oligocene, Eocene, Paleocene, early_Cretaceous, late_Cretaceous, early_Jurassic, middle_Jurassic, late_Jurassic, early_Triassic, middle_Triassic, late_Triassic,
Cisuralian, Guadalupian, Lopingian, Carboniferous, Llandovery, Wenlock, Ludlow, Pridoli, early_Ordovician, middle_Ordovician, late_Ordovician, Ediacaran, Cryogenian, Tonian, Stenian, Ectasian, Calymmian, Statherian,
Orosirian, Rhyacian, Siderian) in total. 
Cited from (Gradstein, F. M., Ogg, J. G., Schmitz, M. D., & Ogg, G. M. (Eds.). (2012). The geologic time scale 2012. elsevier.)
